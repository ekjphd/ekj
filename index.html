Emily K. Johnson, Ph.D.

Associate Professor of English · University of Central Florida

Welcome! On this site you'll find a sampling of my work. It shouldn't take long to notice that I have a wide variety of interests and like finding connections across traditional fields of scholarship.

I am an Associate Professor of English, Interim Associate Chair of the English Department, and core faculty in the Texts and Technology Ph.D. program at the University of Central Florida. My work focuses on technical communication, UX, and digital humanities. A common thread running through my research is learning and fun — I build and study interactive learning environments, from Twine choose-your-own-adventure narratives to immersive language-learning VR games. I do quantitative and qualitative research, and generally favor mixed methods to understand a research question from multiple angles.

A former middle school Language Arts teacher, my guiding philosophy has always been that "learning that's fun is fun." When learners are actively engaged, they learn more effectively. My middle schoolers were my first playtesters, and they're what first drew me toward interactive media and its potential for communication and learning. That thread continues in my work today as I explore how multimedia — including video games — can expand technical communication: the more ways we have to communicate complex information, the broader the audience we can reach.

My games and research have come a long way since early classroom experiments with role play and PowerPoint hyperlinks. BeadED Adventures uses a Makey Makey controller and jars painted with conductive paint to build a one-of-a-kind interface for a STEM learning adventure in Twine. With a number of UCF undergraduates, I've developed several platforms of ELLE the EndLess LEarner, a second-language-acquisition game suite playable on mobile, AR, PC, and VR. ELLE currently teaches Portuguese vocabulary but can accommodate nearly any language.

Books

Critical Making in the Age of AI

Johnson, E.K. & Salter, A. (2025). Amherst College Press. Open access.

Coauthored with Anastasia Salter, this open-access book explores how critical making methods can be used to critique, question, and celebrate different aspects of humanity, with example assignments and artifacts from our undergraduate and graduate courses.

Playful Pedagogy in the Pandemic: Pivoting to Game-Based Learning

Johnson, E.K. & Salter, A. (2022). Routledge.

Also coauthored with Anastasia Salter, this book discusses the pros, cons, and potential of game-based, playful learning in the classroom.
